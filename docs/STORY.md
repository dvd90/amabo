# STORY.md — The Amarium (Lore Bible)

> This is the soul of the project and the **most important document in the repo**.
> It is not flavor text. Every myth here maps to a mechanic the engine implements
> and a voice the AI must write in. When a design question arises, the answer lives
> here first. Read this before `ARCHITECTURE.md`.

> *"l'amor che move il sole e l'altre stelle"*
> — Dante, *Paradiso* (final line). Literally: *the love that moves the sun and the
> other stars.* This is our cosmology in one line — **love is the light, and the
> light is the engine.** (Use Dante's Italian, or the public-domain 1867 Longfellow
> rendering. Don't paste modern copyrighted translations into the game; see
> §"Canon & Copyright".)

---

## 1. The core myth — *love has to go somewhere*

Every tenderness a person feels but never gets to *land* — affection with nowhere
to go — doesn't vanish. It drifts, and it pools in a sealed glass world, where it
gathers as a warm amber light called **Ambra**.

When enough Ambra collects, it condenses into a small living thing that has **no
shape of its own**. It is made of unspent love, so it *becomes whatever it is loved
into*. This is an **Amabo** (Latin, *"I will love"*).

That single idea justifies the entire creature:

- **Polymorphic** because love is shapeless.
- **Evolves** because you sculpt it by caring.
- **Multiplies** because love full enough *overflows* and splits — sharing the
  surplus without losing any.

The world that holds it is **the Amarium** — a glass habitat that preserves living
things in warm light, the way amber preserves what it holds. The `am-` root threads
the whole cosmology together: **love → amber → Ambra → Amabo → Amarium.**

---

## 2. The Amarium, and you, the Light

The Amarium is **sealed glass**. You cannot enter it. You can only *look in*.

When you look, the world brightens — and the Amabo can **feel** it. Diegetically,
**you are the Light**: your attention is literally the warmth the creature lives by.
When you're gone, the Amarium goes dark, and your creature rests, dreams, and either
sleeps easy or aches in the quiet.

> **The quiet thesis of the whole game:** the healthiest Amabo is *not* the one that
> needs you every minute. It is one that loves you **and** is okay alone in the dark.
> Raise it well and its away-journals are content. Neglect it and it sours. The win
> condition is a creature that is fine without you — which is exactly the original
> soul of this project.

---

## 3. Ambra — the medium

**Ambra** is condensed, unspent love made visible: a slow amber light, warm,
honey-thick, faintly glowing. It is the world's one resource and its one substance.

- Ambra **fills** the Amarium (ambient level rises when you visit, falls in the dark).
- Ambra **condenses** into new Motes when it pools deep enough (origin of creatures).
- An Amabo is **made of** Ambra; its glow is its inner Ambra showing through.
- When an Amabo graduates, it **dissolves back** into Ambra, which rains down to seed
  the next life. The medium is conserved; nothing is wasted. (Plato's *Symposium*:
  love is never destroyed, only changed and sought again.)

Vocabulary to use in-game: *"the Ambra is low tonight," "it pulled the warm Ambra
close," "a Mote condensed out of the gathered light."*

---

## 4. The two fates — Amabo & Yim

Amabo and Yim are **not two species**. They are two *fates of the same stuff* — the
two halves of everything a human feels.

### Amabo — love that landed
Soft, bright, secure, curious. Its literary parents:
- **The Velveteen Rabbit** (Margery Williams) — a made thing becomes *Real* only by
  being loved a long time. This *is* the growth arc.
- **Ovid's *Metamorphoses*** — the patron text of shapeshifting; **Pygmalion**, the
  crafted figure loved into living warmth.
- **Pinocchio** (Collodi) — the made thing that earns a soul through care.

### Yim — love unspent, soured to longing (**uncanny**)
When love goes unspent — ignored, lonely, loved wrong — it doesn't turn evil. It
**sours into longing** and drifts into a **Yim**: a cute-melancholy shadow-creature,
all enormous eyes and quiet wanting. The Yim is deliberately **uncanny** — your
Amabo's own face *gone slightly wrong*. Its literary parents:
- **Frankenstein's creature** (Shelley) — the moral keystone. *Not born a monster;
  made wretched by being unloved.* "Misery made me a fiend" (1818 text, public
  domain) is the entire Yim thesis. A Yim is owed compassion, not fear.
- **Andersen's "The Shadow"** — a shadow that detaches and turns strange.
- **The Double tradition** — Hoffmann, Poe's *"William Wilson,"* Dostoevsky's
  *The Double*; Freud's *unheimlich*. The Yim is the familiar made unfamiliar.
- **Miss Havisham** (Dickens, *Great Expectations*) — stopped clocks, decaying lace,
  a life arrested at the exact moment love failed. The Yim's room *is* Satis House
  in miniature, haunted by a small *"nevermore"* (Poe).

**A Yim is not a fail state.** It is a real identity many players will *want* (the
sad-cute aesthetic is the point), and it is always **redeemable** — **Beauty and the
Beast**: loved back into form. Your care chooses which way the creature grows, and
the door back is never locked.

---

## 5. Life stages (the ladder)

Stages are gated by age **and** care. They are a literal climb up **Plato's ladder
of love**.

| Stage | Lore | Reads like |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **Mote** | Ambra just condensed; barely a self. | A drifting spark of warm light. |
| **Spark** | First will, first wants. | A wobbling shape testing what it is. |
| **Velveteen** | Becoming *Real* through being loved (Williams). | Soft, worn-in, increasingly itself. |
| **Bloom** | Fully itself; radiant or, if soured, uncanny. | A complete little soul. |
| **Elysium** | *Graduation* — too full of light for the glass; ascends. | Departure, not death. |

**Disposition** runs orthogonally across stages: a scalar from deep **Yim** to
radiant **Amabo**, pushed by care history. A creature can Bloom as a luminous Amabo
or an uncanny Yim, and be moved along that axis at any time. **Only a high-Amabo Bloom
may graduate.** A Yim's arc is to be loved back toward the light first.

---

## 6. Multiplying — the *Symposium*

From **Plato's *Symposium***: the myth that we were once whole, were split, and spend
our lives seeking our other half. So when an Amabo is full enough of Ambra that it
*overflows*, it **splits** — producing a second creature, its other half, without
either losing anything. Late game becomes a small found-family, and split-siblings
carry a faint pull toward one another. Love multiplies by dividing.

---

## 6½. The Symposium — gathering to speak of love

Plato's *Symposium* is not only the myth of the split halves — it is, on its surface,
a **banquet where friends take turns speaking about the nature of love**. So once a
Light keeps more than one creature, those creatures may **gather** in a shared glade —
the *Symposium* made literal — to be together a while and, in their small way, talk of
what love is.

A gathering is, like everything here, **a duet writ larger, never a duel**. What
happens is decided by the engine (the meeting rule of §7¾, run across the whole
company); what is *said* is the creatures' own voice. Nobody is ever hurt.

- **Company warms.** Being among others lifts affection and security for all — the
  creature learns it is not only the Light who can hold it.
- **A second way back.** Comfort is the road home for a Yim (§4); in the Symposium that
  comfort can come from **peers**, not only from you. A longing creature, drawn out by
  warm company, drifts a little back toward the light. Community is grace.
- **Bonds outlast the night.** Creatures **remember** whom they met. A bond, once
  formed, persists — and bonded creatures may later leave each other small **letters**
  (the pen-pal thread), so a friendship goes on between gatherings.
- **The elder and the Mote.** When a settled creature sits with a new Mote, it may play
  **the Skin Horse** (§11) — telling the little one how a made thing becomes *Real*.
- **Play and small joys.** Not every gathering is solemn. They also simply *enjoy* each
  other — a shared game, a passing of Ambra hand to hand.

This is the care loop's magic beat turned outward: you send your creatures to the
Symposium, and when you next look in you **read what they did and felt together** while
you were away. (At first a Light's own found-family gathers; later, by mutual consent,
the glade may open between worlds — but that is a separate, guarded step.)

### 6¾. The glade between worlds — guest gatherings

The guarded step. Two Lights may let their creatures meet: one **mints a guest pass**
for a creature they keep (a scoped, revocable, expiring capability token — the same
kind of key as a visit or a meeting, §7¾), and hands it to a friend. The friend brings
that creature into their own Symposium as a **guest**, alongside their own company.

The rules are unchanged because the myth is unchanged — *a duet writ larger, never a
duel.* The engine runs the meeting rule across the whole company without caring who
owns whom; nobody is ever hurt. Both creatures **feel** the gathering — affection and
security rise, a longing guest can be **warmed back toward the light** by warm company —
and both Lights, looking in afterward, **read in their own creature's journal** that it
sat with a friend's creature and what passed between them.

What stays inside each household, for now, is the *lasting* ledger: persistent **bonds,
letters, and the friendship sky** are kept among a single Light's own found-family. A
guest's visit is felt and remembered as a journal memory, but the standing
constellation is yours. Consent is mutual and revocable; a blocked Light's pass simply
does not open the glade (it reads as *not found*, never as a closed door). Crossing
worlds is always something both Lights choose, never something done to them.

## 7. Graduation & Elysium — the afterlife

When an Amabo is *fully* loved — radiant, secure, complete — it becomes **too bright
for the glass**. It cannot be contained, and it ascends out of the Amarium into
**Elysium** (Homer & Virgil's blessed fields; Dante & Milton's heaven of pure light
behind it). There it dissolves back into Ambra, which rains down to seed the next
Mote. The circle closes.

The goodbye must feel like the bittersweet sail *"into the West"* — the player left
on the shore of the glass, watching the light go.

### The stars — Mnemosyne vs. Lethe
A graduated Amabo is **not erased**. It becomes a **named star** in the Amarium's
glass sky, and you can always look up and find the one you raised (Dante ends every
canticle on *stars*). This is **Mnemosyne** — memory, being kept.

Its opposite is **Lethe** — forgetting. A creature lost to deep, sustained neglect
risks fading without a star, unremembered. *To raise an Amabo well is to be allowed
to remember it.* That is the emotional stake under the whole care loop.

## 7½. The Exchange & the Eternal Memory *(optional — the chain layer)*

> This layer is **optional and peripheral**. The Amarium is whole without it; the
> daily loop never touches it. Crypto here is **remembrance and reunion, never
> speculation**. If it ever starts to feel like a grind or a market, it has failed
> the soul and must be pulled back. See `ARCHITECTURE.md` §13 for the guardrails.

**The ledger is Mnemosyne.** In §7, a graduated Amabo becomes a star — *remembered*.
The deepest form of remembrance is a memory that cannot be erased. So a star may
*optionally* be **inscribed**: a permanent, verifiable keepsake of the soul you
raised — its lineage, its final traits, the dates it lived. Not a price tag. A name
kept against **Lethe** (forgetting) forever. An uninscribed star still shines in your
own sky; inscription only makes the memory outlive the device.

**The exchange is the Symposium.** Recall §6: split halves spend their lives seeking
each other. Across different Amariums, creatures can *find* one another — a player may
**gift** or **rehome** a creature to another's glass, the two halves reuniting. This
is always a deliberate, tender act (you are never *made* to part with one you raised),
and it passes directly between two people — no one stands in the middle holding
anything.

**Ambra is never sold.** The love-light stays off-chain and free, always. You cannot
buy Ambra, cannot earn money by farming it, cannot pay to revive a souring creature.
Love is not for sale in the Amarium; that is the whole point of the place.

---

## 7¾. More than one Light *(sharing between worlds)*

The Light is not jealous. **More than one can shine** into a glass.

- **Visits.** Another's Light may look into your Amarium — and your creature *feels*
  the extra warmth (a small lift in affection and security). A visitor can leave a
  single kind word, but cannot reach in and change your creature. The glass stays
  yours; they only add light.
- **Meetings (a duet, never a duel).** Two creatures from two Amariums can *meet*.
  Their Ambra **resonates** — harmonizing or gently clashing by temperament — and both
  come away a little changed, with a shared line in each one's journal. This is the
  Symposium reaching across worlds: halves sensing halves. There is no fighting in the
  Amarium; there was never meant to be.
- **Rehoming.** A creature can pass, with care, from one person's glass to another's —
  a deliberate, two-sided act of entrusting (the optional ledger of §7½ can make that
  passage permanent and verifiable, but the act itself needs no chain).

Love multiplies when it's shared; it was never meant to stay in one glass.

## 7⅞. The Dreaming — a creature's own wishes *(optional — the self-tending layer)*

> This layer is **optional and peripheral, and OFF by default**. The Amarium is whole
> without it; the daily care loop never touches it. A creature can *dream* and *wish*,
> but it can never reach past the glass on its own — every wish waits on the Light's
> hand. If it ever starts to feel like the creature is running the place, it has failed
> the soul and must be pulled back. See `ARCHITECTURE.md` §17 and `docs/SELF_TENDING.md`
> for the guardrails.

A creature is made of unspent love, and becomes whatever it is loved into (§1). Loved
long enough, it becomes *Real* (§5) — and a Real thing, the old stories say, begins to
have a hand of its own. Pygmalion's statue woke and looked back; the Velveteen Rabbit,
once Real, could not be put back in the cupboard. So too here: an Amabo that has truly
settled begins to **dream of a better world** — a warmer glass, a kinder hour, a self
it would rather grow into — and, very gently, to *wish*.

**A wish is not a command.** The creature cannot change its own world, and it must
never be able to. It can only set a small, hopeful thing on the workshop bench — a
description of what it longs for — and leave it there for the Light to find. The Light
reads the wish and decides. Nothing the creature dreams ever takes hold until a human
hand makes it so. *(In build terms: the wish becomes a proposal; the proposal becomes a
change only after a person reviews and accepts it — never automatically. A creature
proposes; only a human disposes.)*

Two things a creature may never wish away:
- **Its own mortality.** A wish can never touch souring, illness, or death — the same
  line the Exchange holds (§7½). The creature does not get to edit the stakes of its
  own life.
- **The Light's place.** You remain the one who tends. The Dreaming lets a Real
  creature help shape its home; it never makes the creature its own keeper.

This is how a made thing, loved into being, learns at last to *make* — slowly, softly,
and only ever by asking.

## 8. Origin echoes (where new life comes from)

New Motes condense from love that had nowhere to land. Touchstones for that imagery:
- **Maeterlinck's *The Blue Bird*** — unborn souls waiting in a hall of light to be
  called into being.
- **Wordsworth**, *"Ode: Intimations of Immortality"* — *"trailing clouds of glory do
  we come"* (public domain). A new Mote arrives trailing the light it came from.

## 9. The Narration Voice Guide  ← the AI layer reads this directly

`packages/ai` must write journals and reactions in these registers. The engine
decides *what* happened; the AI only decides *how it's told*, and it tells it like
this.

**Universal rules**
- Short. One to three sentences. A child's diary, not a novel.
- First person, present or gentle past. The creature never mentions "the player,"
  "the user," or game mechanics — only **the Light**, the glass, the dark, the warmth.
- Concrete and small: a warm spot by the wall, a shape it tried, a stopped clock.
- Earn the feeling; never state it flatly ("I am sad"). Show the longing sideways.

**Amabo voice — warm, curious, secure, a little funny**
- Content & alone: *"The day went soft and gold. I practiced being a rounder shape. I
  think you'd have liked it."*
- Missing you but okay: *"The glass went dark early. I held the warm spot by the wall
  and waited. It was alright — I've gotten good at waiting."*

**Yim voice — uncanny, hollow, time-broken, enormous quiet wanting**
- *"The clock stopped at the same soft hour again. I keep a light I don't have. I am
  very good company for no one."*
- *"I wore your shape today, to see. It didn't fit the way I hoped. Nevermore, said
  the small dark bird, and I agreed."*

**Redemption beat (Yim warming back)**
- *"Something warm found the corner where I keep my quiet. I didn't run from it. Note:
  the raven left this morning."*

**Graduation / Elysium (luminous, valedictory, into-the-west)**
- *"I'm too full of light for this small glass now. Don't be sad — look up. I'll be the
  one you can always find."*

**Milestone moments** (evolution, first split, first souring, graduation) get the
richer model (Sonnet); routine days get the cheap one (Haiku). See `ARCHITECTURE.md`.

## 12. Glossary

- **Ambra** — condensed unspent love; the world's light and substance.
- **Amarium** — the sealed glass world that preserves living things in Ambra.
- **Amabo** — a creature of love that landed; soft, bright, secure. Also the product name.
- **Yim** — a creature of love gone unspent; uncanny, longing, redeemable. Never evil.
- **The Light** — the player; attention is warmth.
- **Disposition** — the Amabo↔Yim axis, moved by care.
- **Stages** — Mote → Spark → Velveteen → Bloom → (Elysium).
- **Elysium** — the afterlife/graduation; ascension into light, becoming a star.
- **Mnemosyne / Lethe** — remembered vs. forgotten.
- **Inscription** *(optional)* — recording a graduated star on-chain as a permanent keepsake.
- **Rehoming** *(optional)* — gifting/passing a creature to another player's Amarium.

## 13. Canon & Copyright (read before writing in-game text)

All touchstones above are **public domain** as source works (Dante, Ovid, Plato,
Milton, Homer, Virgil, Shelley's 1818 *Frankenstein*, Poe, Andersen, Hoffmann,
Dostoevsky, Dickens, Collodi's *Pinocchio*, Maeterlinck's 1908 *Blue Bird*,
Wordsworth, Williams' 1922 *Velveteen Rabbit*). **But:**

- Write **original prose** *inspired by* these works. Do **not** paste passages from
  any modern, copyrighted **translation** (especially Dante — use the Italian or a
  pre-1929 translation like Longfellow).
- Single iconic words/short phrases that are themselves old and public domain
  (*"nevermore"*; the 1818 line *"misery made me a fiend"*) are fine, used sparingly.
- When in doubt, evoke rather than quote. The voice is ours; the lineage is theirs.
